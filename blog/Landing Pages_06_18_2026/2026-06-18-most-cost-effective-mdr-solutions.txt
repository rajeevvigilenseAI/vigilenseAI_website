<section class="bg-light">
<h2>How to Choose the Most Cost-Effective MDR (5 Steps)</h2>
<div class="step-container">
<div class="step-card">
<h3>Step 1: Audit Your Current Data Storage</h3>
<p>Identify where your logs currently live—whether it's Snowflake, Elasticsearch, S3, or BigQuery. Traditional MDRs require you to move this data, which triggers massive egress and ingestion costs.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>List all cloud and on-premise data sources.</li>
<li>Calculate current monthly data egress costs.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="step-card">
<h3>Step 2: Evaluate AI Investigation Capabilities</h3>
<p>Look for a solution that does more than just alert. A cost-effective MDR must triage and correlate alerts across identity, network, and endpoints automatically to save human hours.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Ask if the AI correlates across 50+ sources.</li>
<li>Verify if the tool provides a full audit trail.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="step-card">
<h3>Step 3: Eliminate "Per-Gigabyte" Pricing</h3>
<p>Pricing models based on data volume are not scalable for growing businesses. Choose a vendor that queries your data where it lives, ensuring your security bill doesn't explode as your company grows.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Request a "zero ingestion" pricing model.</li>
<li>Avoid long-term vendor lock-in contracts.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="step-card">
<h3>Step 4: Assess Deployment Speed</h3>
<p>Time-to-value is a critical component of cost-effectiveness. A solution that takes six months to deploy is six months of unprotected risk and wasted budget.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Target a "Live in Days" deployment goal.</li>
<li>Ensure the tool works on your existing infrastructure.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="step-card">
<h3>Step 5: Implement Human-in-the-Loop Response</h3>
<p>While AI handles the bulk of the work, ensure there is a mechanism for human intervention when it matters most. This hybrid approach is the gold standard for midsize business security.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Set up automated containment for known threats.</li>
<li>Establish clear notification paths for your internal team.</li>
</ul>
</div>
