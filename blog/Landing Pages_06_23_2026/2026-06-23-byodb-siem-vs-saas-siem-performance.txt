<section id="faq-cluster">
<h2>What is the difference between BYODb and SaaS SIEM?</h2>
<p>A SaaS SIEM requires you to copy and move your logs into the vendor's cloud, where they charge you per gigabyte. A BYODb SIEM, like Vigilense AI, queries the data where it already lives, providing better performance and zero ingestion fees.</p>

<h2>Why is query performance faster in a BYODb architecture?</h2>
<p>Query performance is faster because BYODb architectures leverage high-performance data warehouses like Snowflake or BigQuery that are optimized for massive scale. Traditional SaaS SIEMs often experience "noisy neighbor" issues in multi-tenant environments.</p>

<h2>How does data residency impact SIEM performance?</h2>
<p>Data residency ensures that your security logs remain in your specific region (e.g., US, UK, EU). By keeping data local, you reduce the time it takes for AI engines to analyze alerts, as there is no cross-region data transit latency.</p>

<h2>Are SaaS SIEM ingestion fees avoidable?</h2>
<p>Yes. By using a BYODb model, you bypass the "tax" on your own data. You only pay for the detection and response intelligence, not the storage of the logs you already own.</p>

<h2>What are the security benefits of keeping data in-house?</h2>
<p>Keeping data in-house minimizes the "attack surface." When you send logs to a SaaS provider, you create a secondary repository of sensitive information that could be breached. BYODb keeps the data under your existing IAM and encryption protocols.</p>

<h2>Can BYODb SIEMs handle high-volume logs like VPC Flow?</h2>
<p>Absolutely. Because BYODb leverages your existing cloud storage (S3, Azure Blob), it can ingest terabytes of VPC flow and DNS logs that would be cost-prohibitive in a SaaS SIEM model.</p>

<h2>Which SIEM model is best for compliance in the UK and EU?</h2>
<p>The BYODb model is superior for GDPR and UK Data Protection Act compliance because it ensures data never leaves the sovereign jurisdiction of the organization.</p>

<h2>How long does it take to deploy a BYODb SIEM vs SaaS?</h2>
<p>Vigilense AI can be live in days. Since the data is already in your infrastructure, we simply connect our AI engine to your existing data source, whereas SaaS SIEMs require complex API integrations and data forwarding configurations.</p>

<section>
<h2>How to Choose the Right SIEM Model (5 Steps)</h2>

<div class="how-to-step">
<h3>Step 1: Audit Your Current Data Volume</h3>
<p>Determine how many gigabytes of logs your organization generates daily across endpoints, identity providers, and cloud networks. SaaS SIEM costs scale linearly with this number, while BYODb costs remain flat.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Check your monthly cloud egress and storage bills.</li>
<li>Identify high-volume sources like Firewall and VPC logs.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="how-to-step">
<h3>Step 2: Evaluate Data Residency Requirements</h3>
<p>Identify where your data must legally reside. If you operate in the US, UK, or EU, keeping data in your local Snowflake or S3 bucket simplifies compliance tremendously.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Review GDPR, SOC2, or HIPAA requirements.</li>
<li>Verify if your current vendor moves data across borders.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="how-to-step">
<h3>Step 3: Test Query Performance with Real-World Scenarios</h3>
<p>A SIEM is only useful if it can search historical data quickly during an incident. Compare the speed of a "Select All" query on a SaaS platform versus your own data warehouse.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Run a test query for a specific IP address over 30 days.</li>
<li>Measure the "Time to First Result."</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="how-to-step">
<h3>Step 4: Assess AI &amp; Automation Capabilities</h3>
<p>Performance isn't just about data speed; it's about the speed of response. Ensure your SIEM can automatically triage alerts so your team isn't buried in noise.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Look for "Human-in-the-loop" AI investigation features.</li>
<li>Check for 50+ pre-built data source integrations.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="how-to-step">
<h3>Step 5: Calculate Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)</h3>
<p>Compare the long-term costs. SaaS SIEMs often start cheap but become prohibitively expensive as your business grows. BYODb provides a predictable, fixed-cost model.</p>
<ul class="action-items">
<li>Project your data growth over the next 3 years.</li>
<li>Factor in the cost of hiring a 24/7 SOC team versus AI-powered MDR.</li>
</ul>
</div>
