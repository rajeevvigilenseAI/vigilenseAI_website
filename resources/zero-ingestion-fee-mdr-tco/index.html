How to Choose the Right Zero-Ingestion MDR (5 Steps)

Ready to lower your security costs without sacrificing protection? Follow this roadmap to deploy an AI-driven SOC workflow.

Step 1: Identify Your Existing Data Lakes

Before selecting an MDR provider, audit where your logs currently reside. If you are already using Snowflake, BigQuery, or AWS S3, you shouldn't pay to move that data elsewhere.

Action items:
  • List all log sources (Endpoints, Identity, Network).
  • Confirm current cloud storage costs.

Step 2: Evaluate In-Place Query Capabilities

Ask potential vendors if they can query data "in-place." If they require you to install a heavy collector that replicates data to their proprietary cloud, you will face ingestion fees.

Action items:
  • Request a technical demo of the query engine.
  • Verify support for 50+ data sources.

Step 3: Automate Alert Triage with AI

The biggest hidden cost in MDR is the human labor for triage. Ensure the platform uses AI to correlate endpoints, identity, and threat intel in minutes, not hours.

Action items:
  • Test the AI's ability to reduce false positives.
  • Ensure human-in-the-loop options are available for critical responses.

Step 4: Configure Automated Containment

Detection is only half the battle. Your MDR must be able to contain threats automatically, creating tickets and notifying your team with a full audit trail for compliance.

Action items:
  • Map out response playbooks for ransomware and phishing.
  • Set up notifications for your existing IT team.

Step 5: Calculate and Monitor TCO

Once live, monitor the total cost of ownership. With zero ingestion fees, your budget should remain predictable even as your data volume grows.

Action items:
  • Review monthly storage vs. licensing spend.
  • Audit the time saved by the AI-powered SOC workflow.
