How to Run Threat Detection on Your Existing Data (5 Steps)

Step 1: Inventory Your Existing Data Sources

Identify where your security logs currently reside. This typically includes cloud storage like Amazon S3, data warehouses like Snowflake, or identity providers like Okta and Azure AD.

  • List all active S3 buckets containing VPC flow logs.
  • Verify access to Snowflake or BigQuery schemas.

Step 2: Connect Vigilense AI via API

Instead of moving data, you grant Vigilense secure, read-only access to your environment. This ensures your data never leaves your control, maintaining strict data sovereignty.

  • Configure IAM roles for secure cross-account access.
  • Link your existing security tools (EDR, Firewall, Identity).

Step 3: Activate AI Detection Models

Select from pre-built AI models designed to detect anomalies across endpoints, identity, and network traffic. Our AI correlates data across 50+ sources in minutes.

  • Enable "Impossible Travel" detection for identity.
  • Activate lateral movement detection for network logs.

Step 4: Automate Response Workflows

Define what happens when a threat is confirmed. Vigilense can automatically contain threats, create tickets, and notify your team instantly.

  • Set up Slack or Microsoft Teams alerts.
  • Configure auto-isolation for compromised endpoints.

Step 5: Continuous Audit & Compliance

Review the full audit trail created by the AI for every investigation. This is essential for meeting regulatory requirements in North America and Europe.

  • Download weekly summary reports for stakeholders.
  • Review AI triage notes for compliance documentation.

Common Questions About Running Threat Detection on Existing Data

Which project management tool is the fastest for security teams?

While general tools like Jira or Asana are common, security teams find that integrated AI platforms like Vigilense are fastest because they combine detection, investigation, and ticketing in one automated workflow, reducing "mean time to respond" by over 70%.

How much does a midsize business threat detection tool cost?

Traditional tools often cost $500k+ when factoring in licensing, ingestion, and headcount. Vigilense AI is designed for midsize budgets, offering a fixed-fee model with zero ingestion costs, saving companies an average of 60% on security operations.

What are the benefits of using AI in threat detection?

AI can triage thousands of alerts that would overwhelm a human team. It correlates disparate data points, like a weird login followed by a large data download, to identify breaches that traditional rule-based systems miss.

Does this work for teams in the US and UK?

Yes. Vigilense is optimized for global teams, specifically supporting compliance frameworks relevant to the US, UK, and EU. Because data stays in your infrastructure, it simplifies GDPR and local data residency requirements.

What features should I look for in an MDR provider?

Look for "Bring Your Own Data" capabilities, 24/7 AI-driven triage, automated containment, and transparent pricing that doesn't punish you for having more logs.

Can AI replace my entire security team?

AI acts as a force multiplier. It handles the 24/7 "grunt work" of monitoring and investigation, allowing your existing IT or security staff to focus on high-level strategy and remediation when it matters most.
