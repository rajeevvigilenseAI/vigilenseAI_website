How to Implement AI Security for Your Clients (5 Steps)

Transitioning to an AI-powered detection and response model is straightforward when you follow this proven framework designed for MSSPs and midsize enterprises.

Step 1: Inventory Your Existing Data Infrastructure

Before deploying AI, identify where your client's security logs currently reside. Whether they use Snowflake, S3, or OpenSearch, the best AI security solutions should integrate with these without requiring a migration.

  • Action items: List all log sources (Endpoints, Identity, Network) and verify cloud storage compatibility.

Step 2: Connect the AI SOC Workflow

Deploy Vigilense AI to sit on top of your existing infrastructure. This allows the AI to monitor the environment 24/7, triaging every alert and correlating data across 50+ sources in minutes.

  • Action items: Configure API access and define the scope of the 24/7 monitoring environment.

Step 3: Define Automated Response Playbooks

Determine which threats should be contained automatically. For example, if the AI detects an unauthorized identity login, it can automatically suspend the account or isolate the endpoint while notifying your team.

  • Action items: Select "Human in the loop" thresholds for high-impact actions and automate low-level containment.

Step 4: Establish a Full Audit Trail for Compliance

Ensure every AI investigation is documented. Vigilense creates tickets and a full audit trail, which is essential for meeting regulatory requirements in regions like the US, UK, and EU.

  • Action items: Review generated reports for GDPR, SOC2, or HIPAA compliance alignment.

Step 5: Scale and Optimize

As you add more clients or data sources, the AI continues to learn and optimize. Because there are no ingestion fees, you can increase data visibility without increasing your security bill.

  • Action items: Perform monthly reviews of AI-detected anomalies to refine detection rules.
