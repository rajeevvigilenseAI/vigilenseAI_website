Everything you need to know about evaluating and selecting a modern SIEM

Executive Summary

The Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) landscape has evolved dramatically. Legacy SIEMs built for a bygone era are being replaced by AI-native platforms that deliver autonomous security operations. This guide will help you evaluate SIEM solutions and make the right choice for your organization.

The Evolution of SIEM

First Generation: Log Management (2000s)

Early SIEMs were essentially log aggregators with basic search capabilities. They collected logs from firewalls, servers, and applications, providing a centralized view but requiring significant manual effort to derive insights.

Second Generation: Correlation & Rules (2010s)

The second generation introduced correlation engines and rule-based detection. Analysts could create rules to identify known attack patterns. However, these systems struggled with alert fatigue, false positives, and couldn't adapt to new threats without manual rule creation.

Third Generation: AI-Native SIEM (2020s)

Modern AI-native SIEMs leverage machine learning for detection, investigation, and response. They adapt to new threats automatically, reduce false positives, and can operate autonomously ‚Äî turning the SOC from a cost center into a force multiplier.

Total Cost of Ownership

When evaluating SIEM costs, consider the full picture:

  • Licensing: Per-user, per-GB, or flat pricing models
  • Infrastructure: Cloud vs. on-premise costs
  • Data Ingestion: Volume-based charges can escalate quickly
  • Personnel: How many analysts are needed to operate?
  • Training: Time to proficiency for your team
  • Maintenance: Ongoing tuning and rule management
  • Hidden Costs: Professional services, premium support, add-on features

Questions to Ask Vendors

  1. What percentage of alerts can be automatically triaged without human intervention?
  2. How does your pricing model work as our data volumes grow?
  3. Can we use our existing data infrastructure (BYODb)?
  4. What AI/ML capabilities are included vs. add-on?
  5. How long does typical deployment take?
  6. What's your approach to false positive reduction?
  7. Can we bring our own AI/LLM for data sovereignty?
  8. What compliance certifications do you have?
  9. How do you handle multi-tenant data isolation?
  10. What's the SLA for detection and response times?
