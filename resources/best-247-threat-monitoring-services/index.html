Common Questions About 24/7 Threat Monitoring

How does 24/7 threat monitoring prevent data breaches?

Continuous monitoring ensures that anomalies, such as unauthorized logins or unusual data transfers, are flagged instantly. By using AI to triage alerts 24/7, threats are contained before they can escalate into full-scale breaches.

What is the difference between MDR and traditional threat monitoring?

Traditional monitoring often just alerts you to a problem. Managed Detection and Response (MDR) goes a step further by actively investigating the alert and taking automated or guided actions to stop the attack.

Why do midsize businesses need AI-powered security?

Midsize firms lack the budget for massive human teams. AI scales your security by correlating data across 50+ sources in minutes, doing the work that would take a human analyst hours to complete.

How much do managed detection and response services cost?

Traditional MDR providers charge per gigabyte of data ingested into their cloud. Vigilense AI changes the math by querying your data where it lives, offering a more predictable and lower total cost of ownership.

Can threat monitoring work with Snowflake or AWS S3?

Yes. Modern solutions like Vigilense AI are built to work on top of your existing infrastructure, including Snowflake, Elasticsearch, OpenSearch, S3, and BigQuery, with zero data movement required.

What are the benefits of keeping security data in your own infrastructure?

Keeping data in your own infrastructure ensures compliance, reduces latency, eliminates egress/ingestion fees, and prevents vendor lock-in, giving you full sovereignty over your security logs.

How to Deploy 24/7 Threat Monitoring in 5 Steps

Step 1: Connect Your Existing Data Sources

Vigilense connects directly to where your data already lives, whether that is Snowflake, BigQuery, or an S3 bucket. There is no need to set up complex ETL pipelines or move massive log files.

Action items:
  • Identify primary log storage locations.
  • Grant read-only access to the Vigilense AI engine.

Step 2: Configure AI Detection Rules

Activate pre-built AI models that monitor endpoints, identity providers, and network traffic. Our AI begins learning your environment's baseline immediately to distinguish between normal activity and true threats.

Action items:
  • Select relevant threat intelligence feeds.
  • Define critical assets that require high-priority monitoring.

Step 3: Set Up Automated Response Playbooks

Determine how the system should react when a threat is confirmed. You can choose to contain threats automatically (like disabling a compromised user account) or require a human "thumbs up."

Action items:
  • Choose "Human-in-the-loop" vs. "Full Auto" for different alert levels.
  • Integrate with Slack or Microsoft Teams for notifications.

Step 4: Launch 24/7 AI Triage

The AI engine begins triaging every alert across 50+ sources. Unlike human teams that suffer from alert fatigue, the AI investigates every single anomaly with the same level of rigor, 24/7/365.

Action items:
  • Review initial triage reports in the dashboard.
  • Fine-tune sensitivity based on early results.

Step 5: Continuous Audit and Compliance Reporting

Generate full audit trails for compliance. The system documents every investigation, every piece of evidence, and every response action taken, making your next security audit a breeze.

Action items:
  • Schedule weekly executive security summaries.
  • Export compliance logs for regulatory filings.
