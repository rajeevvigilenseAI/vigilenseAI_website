How to choose and implement an AI MDR (5 Steps)

Step 1: Audit Your Data Sources

Identify where your security logs currently reside. Whether it is Snowflake, Elasticsearch, or S3 buckets, ensure your MDR can query these directly without moving data.

  • List all endpoint, identity, and cloud providers.
  • Check current monthly data ingestion costs.

Step 2: Evaluate AI Investigation Depth

Not all "AI" is equal. Look for providers that do more than just alert; you need a system that correlates 50+ sources to build a full timeline of an attack.

  • Ask for a demo of automated triage.
  • Verify if the AI can handle 24/7 monitoring without human intervention.

Step 3: Define Containment Boundaries

Decide what actions the AI can take automatically versus what requires a human-in-the-loop. This ensures rapid containment while maintaining operational control.

  • Set rules for isolating compromised endpoints.
  • Configure notification triggers for your internal IT team.

Step 4: Verify Data Sovereignty

Ensure the provider adheres to your regional compliance needs. The best providers will never ask you to copy your logs into their cloud.

  • Review the vendor's data movement policy.
  • Confirm zero-vendor-lock-in for your security telemetry.

Step 5: Rapid Deployment & Testing

Modern AI MDR should be functional in days. Connect your existing data lakes and run a simulated attack to verify the detection and response loop.

  • Connect your existing infrastructure via API.
  • Run a "Purple Team" exercise to test AI response.
