Frequently Asked Questions About AI Security

Is an AI SOC more effective than human analysts?

AI excels at processing vast amounts of data without fatigue. While humans are better at creative problem-solving, AI is significantly more effective at triaging thousands of alerts across 50+ sources like endpoints, identity, and network logs in seconds.

What are the benefits of using AI in security operations?

The primary benefits include 24/7 coverage, zero human error in initial triage, massive cost savings, and the ability to scale security as your data grows without hiring more headcount.

Can AI handle incident response autonomously?

Yes. Modern AI SOC analysts can contain threats automatically by isolating hosts or disabling compromised accounts based on pre-defined playbooks, keeping a "human in the loop" only for critical final decisions.

Does an AI SOC require moving data to a third-party cloud?

Traditional MDRs do, but Vigilense AI does not. Our platform queries your data where it lives, Snowflake, Elasticsearch, S3, or BigQuery, ensuring zero data movement and zero ingestion fees.

How much does an AI-powered SOC cost?

Most AI-driven solutions offer a flat-fee model, eliminating the unpredictable "per-gigabyte" costs associated with legacy SIEM and MDR providers.

Is AI security suitable for businesses in the UK and EU?

Absolutely. Because Vigilense keeps data within your own infrastructure, it is the ideal solution for meeting GDPR and local data residency requirements in the UK, Europe, and North America.

How to Implement an AI SOC in 5 Steps

Transitioning from manual logs to an AI-powered detection and response workflow is faster than you think.

Step 1: Audit Your Existing Data Sources

Identify where your security logs currently reside. Whether it's AWS S3, Snowflake, or Google BigQuery, the AI needs to know where to find the evidence.

  • List all cloud storage and database providers
  • Verify access permissions for security logs

Step 2: Connect the AI Engine Without Movement

Integrate Vigilense AI with your existing infrastructure. Unlike legacy tools, this doesn't involve "shipping" logs; it involves granting the AI permission to query them in place.

  • Configure API connectors for 50+ sources
  • Ensure zero ingestion architecture is active

Step 3: Define Automated Response Playbooks

Determine what actions the AI should take when a threat is confirmed. This could include isolating a laptop or revoking an O365 token.

  • Select "Auto-Contain" for high-confidence threats
  • Set up notification channels (Slack, Teams, Email)

Step 4: Establish Human-in-the-Loop Triggers

Decide which scenarios require a human signature before action is taken. This ensures the AI supports your team rather than acting as a "black box."

  • Designate emergency contacts
  • Define escalation paths for complex anomalies

Step 5: Monitor, Audit, and Refine

Review the AI's performance. Use the full audit trail provided for compliance reporting and to fine-tune detection logic for your specific environment.

  • Generate weekly compliance reports
  • Review AI triage accuracy with your team
